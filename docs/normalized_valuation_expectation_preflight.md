## 2. Product decision

AQuantAI will add a structured, append-only layer between accepted source evidence and analyst-owned Investment Candidate valuation/expectation judgments.

```text
accepted evidence / explicit analyst assumption
  -> structured financial observation
  + exact accepted Canonical Price
  -> deterministic normalized valuation metric
  -> deterministic historical or peer context

structured expected observation
  + structured actual observation
  -> deterministic expectation gap
```

The new layer answers:

> What number is reproducibly calculable from exact accepted inputs, is it comparable across time or peers, what did actual results differ from explicit expectations by, and why is a result missing, stale, disputed or non-meaningful?

It does **not** answer:

- what a company is worth;
- what price should be paid;
- expected investment return;
- buy, sell or hold;
- portfolio weight or trade timing.

## 3. Accepted boundaries reused without mutation

### 3.1 v0.6B remains research context

`Stage2ValuationSnapshotRevision` remains analyst-authored valuation context. Its `metric_context`, `observed_value`, `unit`, `currency`, `comparison_basis` and assumptions are not parsed or automatically converted into normalized metrics.

`Stage2MarketExpectationRevision` remains narrative expectation context. Its direction, period horizon and basis do not constitute a numeric expected value.

Both may be linked as exact context revisions when an analyst explicitly records a new structured observation, but neither is a hidden input.

### 3.2 Canonical Price remains authoritative for price

Normalized valuation uses only one exact `CanonicalPriceRevision` whose:

- `canonical_status = accepted`;
- price kind is `official_close`;
- unit is `currency_per_share`;
- adjustment basis is `unadjusted` in v1;
- instrument, currency, trade date and provenance are explicit;
- information cutoff and recorded UTC are visible at the requested boundaries.

The price must also participate in one exact eligible `ComparisonEligibilityRevision` with purpose code:

`normalized_valuation_metric_v1`

V1 does not silently reuse `company_research_price_context_v1` eligibility because the purpose is different.

### 3.3 Investment Candidate scores remain analyst-owned

Existing `valuation_context` and `expectation_gap` component scores remain explicit D3 revisions. New normalized outputs may support a future newly recorded component revision, but they never:

- mutate an existing component revision;
- recompute an existing snapshot;
- create a component score;
- change candidate status or priority automatically.

| Canonical price | Canonical Price | Existing L3/D1 ownership |

## 5. Structured financial observation contract

### 5.1 Stable identity

One `StructuredFinancialObservation` identity is keyed by:

- one exact Company Research identity;
- one exact Listed Instrument identity;
- `metric_code`;
- `source_kind`;
- `target_period_key`;
- `accounting_scope`;
- `currency_code` when applicable;
- `unit_code`.

Corrections append revisions under the stable identity. A compatible-looking newer record is never selected automatically.

### 5.2 Closed metric vocabulary

V1 supports exactly:

1. `diluted_shares_outstanding`
2. `revenue`
3. `net_profit_attributable`
4. `ebitda`
5. `free_cash_flow`
6. `net_debt`

Adding a metric requires a separately reviewed rule version.

### 5.3 Units and currency

| Metric | Unit | Currency |
| --- | --- | --- |
| `diluted_shares_outstanding` | `shares` | null |
| `revenue` | `currency_amount` | required |
| `net_profit_attributable` | `currency_amount` | required |
| `ebitda` | `currency_amount` | required |
| `free_cash_flow` | `currency_amount` | required |
| `net_debt` | `currency_amount` | required |

Input preserves source text and standardized Decimal text. Storage uses sufficient precision for `Numeric(38, 6)`. Parsing accepts ordinary decimal text only. NaN, infinity, binary-float JSON values and exponent overflow fail before persistence.

Rounding is `ROUND_HALF_EVEN`.

### 5.4 Source kind

`source_kind` is exactly:

- `actual`
- `guidance`
- `consensus`
- `research_assumption`

Rules:

- `actual`, `guidance` and `consensus` require exact accepted Claim and Evidence links;
- `research_assumption` requires one exact Company Research revision, analyst rationale and falsification condition;
- Provider name or document title cannot determine source kind;
- an LLM may draft text later but cannot persist or accept a value.

### 5.5 Observation state

`observation_state` is exactly:

- `supported`
- `missing`
- `disputed`
- `rejected`
- `not_applicable`

Only `supported` has a numeric value and may enter arithmetic. No other state is converted to zero.

### 5.6 Period basis and target period

`period_basis` is exactly:

- `instant`
- `ttm`
- `fy_actual`
- `forward_fy1`
- `forward_fy2`

Each revision stores:

- `target_period_key`;
- `period_start_date` where applicable;
- `period_end_date`;
- `fiscal_year` where applicable;
- `observation_as_of_date`;
- `effective_start_date` and optional `effective_end_date` for share-count observations.

Rules:

- shares and net debt use `instant`;
- actual flow metrics use `ttm` or `fy_actual`;
- guidance, consensus and research assumptions may use `forward_fy1` or `forward_fy2`;
- actual and expected observations for expectation-gap calculation share one exact `target_period_key`, even though their source kind and period basis differ;
- period dates cannot be inferred from text such as “next year”.

### 5.7 Accounting scope

`accounting_scope` is exactly:

- `consolidated`
- `consolidated_attributable`

Required mapping:

- revenue, EBITDA, free cash flow and net debt: `consolidated`;
- net profit attributable: `consolidated_attributable`;
- diluted shares: `consolidated_attributable`.

V1 does not convert parent-only, segment, adjusted or non-GAAP values.

### 5.8 Exact provenance

A supported revision freezes:

- one exact Company Research revision;
- one exact Listed Instrument revision;
- exact Claim revisions;
- exact Claim-Evidence links and Evidence items;
- optional exact v0.6B expectation or valuation context revision;
- information cutoff date;
- recorded-at UTC;
- recorded-by actor;
- superseded revision where applicable.

All exact inputs must be visible at both as-of boundaries.

## 6. Capital-structure compatibility

### 6.1 Price date

Every normalized valuation revision has one explicit `valuation_as_of_date`.

The supplied canonical official-close trade date must be:

- on or before the valuation date;
- no more than seven calendar days before the valuation date;
- explicitly supplied, never selected by fallback.

### 6.2 Share-count effective range

The exact diluted-share observation must have an effective range containing the canonical price trade date:

```text
effective_start_date <= trade_date
and
(effective_end_date is null or trade_date <= effective_end_date)
```

This is the v1 corporate-action guard. If an explicit effective range cannot be supported, valuation arithmetic fails closed.

V1 does not detect splits, bonus issues, rights issues or buybacks from price history.

### 6.3 Financial freshness

For TTM and instant denominator inputs used in valuation:

- observation date or period end must be on or before the valuation date;
- maximum age is 120 calendar days;
- the exact age is displayed.

Forward observations may end after the valuation date but must have been recorded and information-visible at the valuation boundaries.

No stale value is silently accepted.

## 7. Normalized valuation identity and revision

### 7.1 Stable identity

One `NormalizedValuationMetric` identity is keyed by:

- one exact Listed Instrument identity;
- `metric_code`;
- `valuation_as_of_date`;
- `target_period_key`;
- `period_basis`;
- `accounting_scope`;
- formula rule version.

Corrections append revisions.

### 7.2 Closed metric vocabulary

- `pe`
- `ps`
- `ev_ebitda`
- `fcf_yield`

### 7.3 Formula inputs

#### PE

Required:

- accepted unadjusted canonical official-close price;
- eligible price assessment for `normalized_valuation_metric_v1`;
- supported effective diluted-shares observation;
- supported net-profit-attributable observation.

```text
equity_value = price * shares
pe = equity_value / net_profit_attributable
```

#### PS

Required:

- price;
- price eligibility;
- shares;
- revenue.

```text
ps = equity_value / revenue
```

#### EV/EBITDA

Required:

- price;
- price eligibility;
- shares;
- net debt;
- EBITDA.

```text
enterprise_value = equity_value + net_debt
ev_ebitda = enterprise_value / ebitda
```

Negative net debt is valid and represents net cash.

#### FCF yield

Required:

- price;
- price eligibility;
- shares;
- free cash flow.

```text
fcf_yield_pct = free_cash_flow / equity_value * 100
```

Negative free cash flow yields a negative numeric percentage and remains explicit.

### 7.4 Decimal rule

- input Decimals retain source and standardized text;
- equity and enterprise value use scale 6;
- division uses a working precision of 28 decimal digits;
- normalized multiple and percentage outputs use scale 4;
- final quantization uses `ROUND_HALF_EVEN`;
- output preserves standardized text and Decimal value;
- strict JSON uses strings for exact decimals.

### 7.5 Calculation state

`calculation_state` is exactly:

- `calculated`
- `calculated_negative`
- `non_meaningful_nonpositive_denominator`
- `non_meaningful_nonpositive_enterprise_value`
- `missing_input`
- `disputed_input`
- `rejected_input`
- `stale_input`
- `ineligible_price`
- `incompatible_input`

Rules:

- PE: profit `<= 0` yields no numeric value and `non_meaningful_nonpositive_denominator`;
- PS: revenue `<= 0` yields no numeric value and the same state;
- EV/EBITDA: EBITDA `<= 0` yields no numeric value; enterprise value `<= 0` yields its dedicated state;
- FCF yield: negative FCF yields a numeric value and `calculated_negative`;
- zero or negative equity value is an integrity failure before write;
- missing, disputed, rejected, stale, ineligible or incompatible inputs never produce a numeric result.

### 7.6 Reason codes

Closed reason codes include:

- `price_not_accepted`
- `price_purpose_ineligible`
- `price_too_old`
- `share_effective_range_mismatch`
- `financial_observation_too_old`
- `currency_mismatch`
- `unit_mismatch`
- `instrument_mismatch`
- `period_mismatch`
- `horizon_mismatch`
- `accounting_scope_mismatch`
- `input_missing`
- `input_disputed`
- `input_rejected`
- `profit_nonpositive`
- `revenue_nonpositive`
- `ebitda_nonpositive`
- `enterprise_value_nonpositive`
- `free_cash_flow_negative`

Reason codes are sorted deterministically and do not imply attractiveness.

## 8. Historical comparison context

### 8.1 Identity

One `ValuationComparisonSet` with `comparison_kind = historical` is keyed by:

- subject instrument;
- normalized metric code;
- period basis and horizon;
- accounting scope;
- formula version;
- purpose code `normalized_valuation_historical_context_v1`.

### 8.2 Frozen membership

A revision freezes:

- one exact current normalized valuation revision;
- every explicitly supplied historical normalized valuation revision;
- member eligibility and reason codes;
- information cutoff and recorded UTC;
- rule version.

No member is replaced by a newer revision.

### 8.3 Compatibility

Members must share:

- instrument identity;
- metric code;
- formula version;
- period basis/horizon;
- accounting scope;
- currency and unit;
- calculation output scale.

Only `calculated` and `calculated_negative` values are numerically eligible. Other members remain visible but are excluded from numeric context.

### 8.4 Minimum sufficiency

Historical percentile is calculated only when:

- at least eight eligible observations exist, including the current observation;
- observations span at least 730 calendar days;
- at least four distinct financial period-end dates exist;
- valuation dates are unique.

Otherwise state is `insufficient_history` and no percentile is emitted.

### 8.5 Percentile formula

For subject value `x` among `n` eligible values:

```text
value_percentile =
  (count(value < x) + 0.5 * count(value = x)) / n * 100
```

Output scale is 2 with `ROUND_HALF_EVEN`.

This is a raw-value percentile:

- high PE percentile means a high PE relative to the frozen history;
- high FCF-yield percentile means a high FCF yield;
- it is never labeled “cheap”, “expensive”, “good” or “bad”.

Median uses the middle value or arithmetic mean of the two middle values. Min, max, median and percentile expose exact member count and excluded-member count.

## 9. Peer comparison context

### 9.1 Peer-set ownership

Peer-set membership is explicit analyst-owned D3 state. It stores:

- purpose and bounded rationale;
- subject company/instrument;
- exact peer Company Research and Listed Instrument revisions;
- membership position for deterministic display;
- information cutoff and recorded UTC.

No industry-code, company-name, market-cap, Provider, text or AI inference creates membership.

### 9.2 Complete membership

A peer comparison revision preserves every explicit peer-set member, including members whose metric is missing, non-meaningful or ineligible.

The explicit input manifest must equal persisted peer membership. Omission, duplication or substitution fails the whole write.

### 9.3 Metric compatibility

Peer numeric context requires:

- same metric code and formula version;
- same period basis, target fiscal year/horizon and accounting scope;
- same currency and unit;
- one common `valuation_as_of_date`;
- each supplied price trade date on/before and within seven days of that date;
- calculated numeric state.

V1 performs no FX conversion.

### 9.4 Minimum sufficiency and output

Peer percentile/median/range requires at least three eligible members including the subject.

All members remain visible. Ineligible members carry reason codes and are excluded only from arithmetic.

The same raw-value percentile formula is used. No ordinal “cheapest company” ranking is persisted. Display order is explicit peer-set position, then instrument UUID.

## 10. Structured expectation gap

### 10.1 Identity

One `NormalizedExpectationGap` identity is keyed by:

- Company Research identity;
- Listed Instrument identity;
- metric code;
- target period key;
- expected source kind;
- rule version.

Revisions append corrections.

### 10.2 Supported metric codes

Expectation gap supports:

- `revenue`
- `net_profit_attributable`
- `ebitda`
- `free_cash_flow`

### 10.3 Exact inputs

A revision freezes:

- one supported expected structured observation with source kind `guidance`, `consensus` or `research_assumption`;
- one supported actual structured observation with source kind `actual`;
- optional exact v0.6B Market Expectation revision as context;
- exact evidence/provenance already frozen by both observations;
- both as-of boundaries.

Expected and actual must share:

- company and instrument;
- metric code;
- target period key and exact period dates;
- currency and unit;
- accounting scope.

### 10.4 Arithmetic

```text
absolute_gap = actual - expected
```

When expected is nonzero:

```text
percentage_gap = (actual - expected) / abs(expected) * 100
```

- working precision: 28 digits;
- amount gap scale: 6;
- percentage gap scale: 4;
- rounding: `ROUND_HALF_EVEN`.

When expected is zero:

- absolute gap is calculated;
- percentage gap is null;
- state includes `percentage_not_meaningful_zero_expected`.

### 10.5 Direction

For the v1 supported metrics, operational direction is:

- `above_expected`
- `below_expected`
- `equal_expected`

All four metrics use `higher_is_above_expected`. This direction is factual relative context, not an investment recommendation. Future cost or leverage metrics require a new rule version.

### 10.6 State and reason codes

`gap_state` is exactly:

- `calculated`
- `percentage_not_meaningful_zero_expected`
- `missing_input`
- `disputed_input`
- `rejected_input`
- `stale_input`
- `incompatible_input`

No hidden consensus lookup, price movement or news volume can create an expectation.

## 11. Additive Investment Candidate integration

The implementation candidate may create one additive table:

`investment_candidate_normalized_metric_links`

Each row links one exact existing Investment Candidate component revision to exactly one of:

- normalized valuation metric revision;
- historical comparison-set revision;
- peer comparison-set revision;
- normalized expectation-gap revision.

`link_role` is exactly:

- `valuation_metric`
- `historical_context`
- `peer_context`
- `expectation_gap`

Database checks enforce exactly one typed target foreign key. A generic UUID plus free-text type is prohibited.

Rules:

- only `valuation_context` components may link valuation/historical/peer context;
- only `expectation_gap` components may link expectation-gap revisions;
- links do not change score, state, verification or snapshot output;
- analysts explicitly append a new component revision before adding links;
- existing component and snapshot history remains untouched.

## 12. Persistence candidate

The minimum additive v1 schema is exactly thirteen tables:

1. `structured_financial_observations`
2. `structured_financial_observation_revisions`
3. `structured_financial_observation_claim_links`
4. `structured_financial_observation_evidence_links`
5. `normalized_valuation_metrics`
6. `normalized_valuation_metric_revisions`
7. `normalized_valuation_metric_input_links`
8. `valuation_comparison_sets`
9. `valuation_comparison_set_revisions`
10. `valuation_comparison_members`
11. `normalized_expectation_gaps`
12. `normalized_expectation_gap_revisions`
13. `investment_candidate_normalized_metric_links`

### 12.1 Input-link design

`normalized_valuation_metric_input_links` owns closed `input_role` values:

- `canonical_price`
- `price_eligibility`
- `diluted_shares`
- `financial_denominator`
- `net_debt`

It contains typed nullable foreign keys to Canonical Price revision, Comparison Eligibility revision or Structured Financial Observation revision, with exactly-one-target and role/target database checks.

Expectation-gap revisions store direct expected/actual observation foreign keys because the pair is cardinality-one and semantically fixed.

### 12.2 Append-only and concurrency

- all thirteen tables are append-only;
- stable identities and revisions commit atomically;
- expected-latest revision number is required;
- same-identity concurrent first revision fails closed;
- no existing table is altered or backfilled;
- downgrade checks all thirteen tables and refuses before dropping any table when any is populated.

### 12.3 Why no generic metrics framework

The schema is domain-specific because:

- financial observation ownership differs from valuation arithmetic;
- peer membership is D3 while percentile arithmetic is D1;
- expectation actual/expected pairing has different compatibility rules;
- Investment Candidate integration cannot own accepted scores.

Shared decimal and append-only utilities may be reused without creating a generic scoring or analytics platform.

## 16. Primary failure path

The transaction fails before any insert when:

- price/instrument/share identity differs;
- price purpose is not eligible;
- currency or unit differs;
- share effective range excludes the price date;
- TTM/instant financial input exceeds 120 days;
- period, horizon or accounting scope differs;
- a required input is inferred from v0.6B free text;
- a peer-set manifest omits, duplicates or substitutes a member;
- expected and actual target-period keys differ;
- any exact input lies outside cutoff or recorded-UTC boundaries;
- expected-latest revision protection fails.

Known non-meaningful denominators are not transaction failures. They persist explicit non-numeric states when all identities and provenance are valid.

## 17. Stop conditions

Implementation must not start if review finds:

- a required structured financial value has no accepted owner;
- the golden path requires Provider/network access;
- currency compatibility requires FX;
- capital-structure compatibility requires hidden corporate-action inference;
- any formula requires parsing free text;
- peer membership must be inferred;
- generic abstraction obscures metric-specific meaning;
- normalized output would automatically mutate Investment Candidate accepted state.
